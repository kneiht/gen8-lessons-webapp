English Conjunctions Practice

Choose the correct conjunctions to complete the sentences

Exercise 1: Choose the correct answers

1. You can have tea, ______ you can have coffee.

2. She was very hungry, ______ she ate a big sandwich.

3. He didn't go to the party, ______ he wasn't feeling well.

4. I like apples, ______ I also enjoy bananas.

5. I was tired, ______ I stayed up late to finish my homework.

6. You must wear your helmet, ______ you will be fined.

7. I studied very hard; ______, I passed the exam with high marks.

8. The weather was rainy; ______, we went hiking.

9. I have an exam tomorrow; ______, I can't go out with you.

10. She was tired; ______, she still helped her mom with the housework.

11. You can go with us, ______ you can stay at home if you like.

12. I was very tired, ______ I determined to walk on to the next village.

13. You can come here either on Monday ______ on Friday.

Exercise 2: Fill in the blanks

Fill in the blanks with the correct conjunction (and, but, or, so, for).

1. Jaewon was cold, ______ he put on a coat.

2. Maria tried to read a novel in French, _____ it was too difficult.

3. To get from Vancouver to Victoria, you can fly, _____ you can ride the ferry.

4. I bought a bottle of wine, _____ we drank it together.

5. The waiter was not very nice, _____ the food was delicious.

6. I went to buy a Rolling Stones CD, ______ the shop didn't have it.

7. Anna needed some money, ______ she took a part-time job.

8. There's so much rain lately! Maybe it's because of El Nino, ______ maybe it's just coincidence.

9. Julie has a guitar, _____ she plays it really well.

10. The concert was cancelled, ______ we went to a nightclub instead.

11. She didn't answer the phone, ______ she was in class.

12. He worked very hard, ______ he wanted to support his family.

13. They went to bed early, ______ they were very tired.

Exercise 3: Choose the correct answers

1. Minh is interested in painting, so / but yesterday he decided to join the arts and crafts club.

2. Life in the city seems to be more comfortable, otherwise / but I prefer life in the countryside.

3. She tries to focus more on her studies; therefore / otherwise, she won't pass her exams.

4. During harvest time, farmers have to get up earlier, and / so they have to work harder.

5. Parents now have higher expectations of their children; therefore / for, children are under more pressure than before.

6. He didn't set an alarm last night, so / otherwise he woke up late for school.

7. Linda loves outdoor activities, but / and she rarely has free time to enjoy them.

8. You should wear a jacket; otherwise / therefore, you will catch a cold.

9. My brother studies engineering, and / but my sister wants to be a doctor.

10. The traffic was terrible; therefore / however, we arrived at the meeting late.
